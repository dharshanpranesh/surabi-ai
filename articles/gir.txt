Gir cattle is an indigenous dairy breed originating from the Gir forest region of Gujarat, India.

Characteristics:
Gir cattle have a distinctive domed forehead, long ears, and curved horns. Their coat color ranges from red to reddish-white.

Milk Production:
Gir cows produce around 1200–1800 kg of milk per lactation. They are considered an important dairy breed in India.

Adaptability:
Gir cattle are highly tolerant to heat and resistant to many tropical diseases.

Uses:
They are mainly used for dairy production and breeding programs.

Maintenance:
Gir cattle require balanced feeding that includes green fodder, dry fodder, and concentrate feed. Clean drinking water should always be available. Proper shelter is required to protect them from extreme weather. Regular vaccination and veterinary checkups are necessary to maintain their health.
