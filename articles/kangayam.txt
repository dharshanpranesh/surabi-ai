Kangayam cattle is a native breed from Tamil Nadu, India, mainly found in the Kangayam region.

Characteristics:
Kangayam cattle are medium-sized animals with a grey coat. Bulls are strong and muscular.

Milk Production:
Milk yield is relatively low, usually around 500–800 kg per lactation.

Adaptability:
They are highly tolerant to hot and dry climates.

Uses:
Kangayam cattle are mainly used for draught work in agriculture.

Maintenance:
Kangayam cattle require simple management and can survive with limited feed. They should be provided with dry fodder, crop residues, and occasional green fodder. Clean water and proper shelter are important for their health.
