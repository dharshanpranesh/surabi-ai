Jersey cattle originated from the Island of Jersey in the English Channel.

Characteristics:
Jersey cattle are small in size with a light brown to dark brown coat.

Milk Production:
Jersey cows produce around 4000–6000 kg of milk per lactation. Their milk has high butterfat content.

Adaptability:
Jersey cattle adapt well to tropical and temperate climates.

Uses:
They are mainly used for dairy farming.

Maintenance:
Jersey cattle require good quality feed including green fodder, concentrates, and mineral mixtures. Clean water and hygienic housing conditions are essential. Regular vaccination and disease control measures should be followed.
